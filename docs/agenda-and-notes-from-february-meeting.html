    • 3.2 1.2 Regression Coefficients
      • 3.2.1 Rule 1: Unspecified relationships among exogenous variables are simply their bivariate correlations.
      • 3.2.2 Rule 2: When two variables are connected by a single path, the coefficient of that path is the regression coefficient.
      • 3.2.3 Rule 3: The strength of a compound path (one that includes multiple links) is the product of the individual coefficients.
      • 3.2.4 Rule 4. When variables are connected by more than one pathway, each pathway is the ‘partial’ regression coefficient.
    • 3.3 Rule 5: Errors on endogenous variables relate the unexplained correlations or variances arising from unmeasured variables.
      • 3.3.1 Rule 6: Unanalyzed (residual) correlations among two endogenous variables are their partial correlations.
      • 3.3.2 Rule 7: The total effect one variable has another is the sum of its direct and indirect effects.
      • 3.3.3 Rule 8: The total effect (including undirected paths) is equivalent to the total correlation.
